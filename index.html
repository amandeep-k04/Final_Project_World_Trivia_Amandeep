The Taj Mahal

Agra, India: An immense mausoleum of white marble, built between 1631 and 1648 by order of the Mughal emperor.

The Himalayas

The "Abode of Snow" contains the planet's highest peaks, including Mount Everest at 8,849 meters.

Canadian Rockies

Banff, Canada: The Rockies are famous for jagged, ice-capped peaks and turquoise lakes like Lake Louise.

Statue of Liberty

New York, USA: A universal symbol of freedom and democracy, dedicated on October 28, 1886.

The Colosseum

Rome, Italy: The largest amphitheater ever built, holding up to 80,000 spectators in ancient times.

Great Wall of China

China: A series of fortifications that were built across the historical northern borders of ancient Chinese states.

Machu Picchu

Peru: A 15th-century Inca citadel, located in the Eastern Cordillera of southern Peru, on a 2,430-meter mountain ridge.

Sydney Opera House

Australia: A multi-venue performing arts centre at Sydney Harbour, famous for its unique shell-like design.

Pyramids of Giza

Egypt: The oldest of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World, and the only one to remain largely intact.
